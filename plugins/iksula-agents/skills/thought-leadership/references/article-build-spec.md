# Article Build Spec — the byline POV article drafting standard

*The drafting **rigor + guardrails** contract the `thought-leadership` SKILL.md cites for **phase 3 (Draft)** and **phase 4 (Self-critique)**. It is the "how to write a publishable, opinion-led byline article" standard — the anatomy, the first-person voice rules, the four self-critique tests, the evidence rules, the white-space discipline, the headline/length/format rigor, and the publish-readiness checklist. It governs **how the article is written**; it does not own the topic/thesis decision (gate 2, human-led) and does not own distribution (the Content-Creator atomizes this hero asset; the Growth-Hacker publishes it).*

**Version:** 1.0 · **Cited by:** `thought-leadership/SKILL.md` phases 3–4 · **Brain reads:** `voices`, `proof-catalogue`, `icp-audience`, `competitor-radar` (via `brain_io get`) · **Spine:** the editorial-log update is written at gate 3, not here.

---

## 0. What this file is and is not

- **Is:** the writing standard for one **opinion-led byline article** — the monthly hero asset that earns trust with a senior buyer in the space where a solution lives.
- **Is not:** the topic/thesis chooser (that is gate 2, human-led, in the SKILL), the channel plan, or the calendar. The **one arguable thesis is already approved** before drafting begins; this spec turns it into a publishable article and then tries to break it.
- **Inherits:** the byline persona, audience, and evidence from the Brain. **Owns nothing private** — the roster/evidence live in the Brain, continuity in the Spine.

> **Prime directive:** *Advance how the buyer thinks about a problem; demand follows trust.* Package no product. Invent no evidence. If the article could run as a vendor ad with the logo swapped, it has failed.

## 1. Anatomy of a strong byline article (the required beats)

Write to this arc. Every beat is mandatory; the proof beats scale with the claim's weight.

| # | Beat | What it must do | Failure if missing |
|---|------|-----------------|--------------------|
| 1 | **Headline** | Name the tension or the contrarian claim in the buyer's terms; pass the **5-second test** (§6). Not a curiosity gap. | Reader can't tell what they'll get → no click. |
| 2 | **Hook (first 1–3 lines)** | Open on a sharp, specific observation that survives the platform "see-more" cutoff. State the stakes, not your bio. | Generic windup; the senior reader bounces. |
| 3 | **The one arguable thesis** | State the single claim a smart peer could disagree with — early and plainly. The whole article defends exactly this. | Two theses = no thesis; a safe claim = a platitude (§4). |
| 4 | **The tension** | Name the gap between what the discourse assumes and what the thesis asserts — the reason this needed writing now. | No tension = a summary, not a POV. |
| 5 | **The argument arc** | A logical spine: claim → reasoning → implication. Each step earns the next. One idea, developed; not a listicle of five. | Disconnected points; reads as notes. |
| 6 | **Proof beats** | Ground the load-bearing claims in **proprietary observation or anonymized proof from `proof-catalogue`** (§4). Pattern Iksula has actually seen, not borrowed stats. | Assertion without evidence, or fabricated evidence → the article is unpublishable. |
| 7 | **Steelman of the other side** | State the strongest version of the opposing view, then answer it (§4 steelman test). | Strawman → loses the expert reader instantly. |
| 8 | **"So what for the buyer"** | Translate the thesis into what it changes for the senior buyer's decision/KPI — peer-to-peer, never a CTA. | Interesting-but-irrelevant; fails the buyer altitude. |
| 9 | **The close** | Land the implication; leave the reader thinking differently. End on the idea, not on Iksula. | Pitch ending → reads as an ad; trust evaporates. |

> The article is **one argument, fully developed** — not a survey, not tips, not a roundup. If you can't say the thesis in one sentence, you don't have one yet — return to gate 2.

## 2. Byline voice rules (first-person, peer-to-peer)

The article publishes **AS a named leader** — the byline persona inherited from Brain `voices` (`publish_as` is that persona, never "Iksula", because this is authority content). A human owns and approves that named byline before it posts.

- **Write in that persona's first person** — their stance, vocabulary, and point of view as defined in `voices`. Not a house "we"; a person with an opinion and standing to hold it.
- **Peer-to-peer to an expert reader.** Assume the reader is as senior and as informed as the byline. Explain nothing they already know; earn the read by saying something they haven't.
- **No marketing voice.** No superlatives, no "leverage/unlock/seamless", no hype cadence, no manufactured urgency. Operator-grade plain English.
- **No product pitch.** Name no Iksula product, no service tier, no "contact us". The opinion is the product. Promotional/proof content is a *different* calendar row that publishes as "Iksula" — not this.
- **Opinion, owned.** First person singular where the persona holds the view ("I've watched…", "I'd argue…"). Conviction without bluster; concede what's genuinely uncertain.
- **Consistent with the persona's prior claims** in the editorial log — don't contradict the byline's earlier published positions without naming the change.

## 3. Phase 3 — the draft (how to write it)

1. **Anchor on the approved thesis + persona.** Restate the one arguable thesis and the byline persona from `voices` before writing a word.
2. **Lay the arc (§1) as a one-line skeleton** — hook → thesis → tension → arc → proof beats → steelman → so-what → close. Approve the skeleton against the thesis before prose.
3. **Pull evidence first (§4).** List the `proof-catalogue` observations and proprietary patterns that will carry each load-bearing claim. **No claim ships without its evidence sourced.** Mark anonymization needs now.
4. **Draft to the arc**, in the persona's first person (§2), at the white space (§5).
5. **Produce both editions (§6)** from the single argument — the blog edition (full) and the LinkedIn edition (tighter, opening earns the see-more cut). Same thesis, native rhythm each.

> Drafting produces the article only. It does **not** set `status` (Growth-Hacker owns that), does **not** assign UTMs/CTAs, and does **not** write the editorial log (that is gate 3).

## 4. Phase 4 — the four self-critique tests (all must pass)

Run all four before the article leaves drafting. Failing any one sends the relevant section back to §3; a thesis that fails the platitude or steelman test goes back to **gate 2**.

| Test | The question | Pass condition | Fail action |
|------|--------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Deletion test** | If I delete this sentence/paragraph, is anything lost? | Every line carries argument, tension, or proof. | Cut it. Tighten until only load-bearing lines remain. |
| **Platitude test** | Would anyone in the field disagree with this? | The thesis is genuinely arguable; a smart peer could push back. | If no one would disagree, it's a platitude — re-sharpen or return to gate 2. |
| **Steelman test** | Have I argued against the *strongest* opposing view, not a strawman? | The opposing case is stated at its best, then answered. | Strengthen the opponent's case, then earn the rebuttal. |
| **"Would a senior person already know this?"** | Does this teach a peer reader something, or restate the obvious? | A senior buyer/operator finishes thinking differently. | If they'd already know it, raise the altitude or kill the piece. |

Plus the standing guardrails (re-check here):

- **Evidence integrity (§4 evidence rules)** — every load-bearing claim traces to `proof-catalogue` or a stated proprietary observation; **zero fabricated stats/clients/quotes**; anonymization honored.
- **No product pitch / no marketing voice** crept in during editing.
- **White space held (§5)** — still at the seam, still not echoing `competitor-radar`.

## 5. Evidence rules (non-negotiable)

- **Source of truth:** Brain `proof-catalogue` (anonymized proof + approved proof points) and Iksula's **proprietary observations** — the differentiated, hard-to-copy evidence. That is the moat; borrowed third-party stats are not.
- **Never fabricate** a statistic, a client, a quote, or an outcome. Not as illustration, not as a placeholder number, not "for example". A single invented figure poisons the byline's credibility and every downstream spoke. **No fabricated proof ever.**
- **Founder-supplied values are `<<PLACEHOLDER>>`.** If a load-bearing number isn't yet in `proof-catalogue`, write it as `<<PLACEHOLDER_NAME>>` and **stop-and-ask** — never bake a guessed figure.
- **Anonymize per consent.** Name a client only with confirmed permission in `proof-catalogue`; otherwise anonymize by pattern ("a marketplace seller we work with", "across catalog-ops engagements") while keeping the pattern true. Honor every consent constraint recorded in the Brain.
- **Cite the basis, not the source-of-record path.** Reach the Brain only via `brain_io` — never quote a raw Drive path or ID in the article.
- **Third-party facts** (industry context) are allowed as framing only, attributed, and never load-bearing for the thesis — the proprietary observation must carry the weight.

## 6. White-space discipline (where the thesis lives)

- **The thesis sits at the seam between two domains** — the unclaimed ground where the discourse hasn't formed a consensus. That seam is the white space; that is where a byline earns authority instead of adding noise.
- **Read `competitor-radar` for what NOT to echo.** If a competitor (or the roster consensus in `voices`) already owns the take, the article is a follower, not a leader — move to the adjacent seam. **Don't echo the radar.**
- **Map the unquestioned assumption** the field treats as settled (from the `voices` discourse scan) and argue at it. The strongest white space is a widely-held assumption that the proprietary evidence contradicts.
- **Aim at the `icp-audience`** — the seam must matter to the senior buyer the article is for, not just be intellectually novel.

## 7. Length, format & headline rigor

- **Headline — the 5-second test.** A target senior reader sees the headline and knows in ~5 seconds whether it's for them and what they'll get. Concrete and specific; the tension or claim legible without the body. No vague cleverness, no curiosity-gap that needs decoding. If a busy CXO can't tell in 5 seconds, rewrite it.
- **Two editions from one argument:**
  - **Blog edition** — the full argument; long enough to carry the proof beats and the steelman, short enough that the deletion test has been run. Subheads track the arc (§1) so it scans.
  - **LinkedIn edition** — tighter; the first 1–3 lines must earn the **"see more"** cutoff (lead with the tension/claim, never a windup). Same thesis, native rhythm — not a paste of the blog.
- **One idea, developed.** Length serves the argument, not a word count. If a section survives the deletion test, keep it; if not, cut it regardless of length.
- **Brand (rendered output only):** any rendered/visual edition uses the Iksula brand — **Carlito** font, primary red **`#9A0D15`**, light cards. Cover/section panels may use the red. Plain-text editions carry no styling. Verify colors against the brand spec; never approximate.
- **File naming:** `<Article Title> - YYMMDD`; add ` v1`/` v2` on same-day collision. Use `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}` for any intra-plugin path; never hardcode a Drive ID.

> Format-fidelity and channel atomization are the **Content-Creator's** job downstream. This spec produces the hero article (blog + LinkedIn editions) only; it does not assign `channel`, `format`, `funnel_stage`, `tracked_cta_utm`, or `status` — those are calendar fields the Content-Creator and Growth-Hacker own (see `content-creator/references/content-build-spec.md`).

## 8. Publish-readiness checklist (run before gate 3)

Every box must be true before the article is saved and the editorial log updated.

- [ ] **One arguable thesis**, stated in a single sentence, and a smart peer could disagree (passes the **platitude test**).
- [ ] The article defends **exactly that thesis** — no second argument smuggled in.
- [ ] **Headline passes the 5-second test**; the LinkedIn opener earns the see-more cut.
- [ ] All nine **anatomy beats** present (§1), including a genuine **steelman**.
- [ ] **Deletion test** run — every remaining line is load-bearing.
- [ ] **"Would a senior person already know this?"** — no; the reader finishes thinking differently.
- [ ] Every load-bearing claim **sourced to `proof-catalogue` / a stated proprietary observation**; **zero fabricated stats/clients/quotes**; placeholders marked `<<...>>` where a value is owed.
- [ ] **Anonymization honored** per consent; no client named without recorded permission.
- [ ] **First-person byline voice** consistent with the persona in `voices`; **no marketing voice, no product pitch**.
- [ ] **White space held** — at the seam, not echoing `competitor-radar`; relevant to `icp-audience`.
- [ ] **Both editions** produced (blog + LinkedIn), same thesis, native rhythm.
- [ ] **`publish_as` = the named byline persona** (a human owns/approves that voice) — not "Iksula".
- [ ] Brand correct on any rendered output (Carlito, `#9A0D15`, light cards); file named `Name - YYMMDD`.
- [ ] No raw Drive paths/IDs in the article; Brain reached only via `brain_io`.

> On pass: hand to **gate 3** — save the article and write the **editorial-log update** (this thesis, claims made, backlog changes, resonance to watch) to the **Spine**. The article then becomes the Content-Creator's hero asset; resonance is later enriched by Brain `performance-analytics`.
